The '''Bark spud''', (also known as a '''Peeling Iron''', '''Peeling Spud''', or abbreviated to '''Spud''') is an implement which is used to remove bark from felled timber. [http://www.americantrails.org/resources/info/tools8.html] Bark peeling tools. [http://www.highlandwoodworking.com/index.asp?PageAction=VIEWPROD&ProdID=7158] Bark Spud

Most bark spuds have [[steel]] heads and wooden handles, typically [[hickory]] or [[ash tree|ash]]. The head is curved, sometimes in one direction with a single cutting edge, and sometimes more dish shaped and sharpened on three sides. In use, the sharpened edge is slid between the bark and the wood, removing the bark from the tree in a number of strips.
